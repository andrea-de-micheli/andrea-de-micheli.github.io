About Me

I am a biomedical engineer in the Responsive Biomedical Systems Lab at ETH Zürich, Swizterland. I currently work on new microtechologies for cancer therapeutics.

I hold a Ph.D. degree from Cornell University and completed my doctoral thesis in the Cosgrove Laboratory. My doctoral work was on the transcriptomic analysis of muscle stem cell heterogeneity in tissue regeneration using single-cell technologies.

Previously, I was a postdoctoral fellow at the Hospital For Special Surgery and a visiting scientist at the Englander Institute For Precision Medicine. There, I developed computational methods to generate single-cell atlases of human clinical muscle samples and of tendon tissues.

I am passionate about biotechnologies and data science for personalized and precision medicine.
